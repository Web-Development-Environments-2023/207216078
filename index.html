Q&A



Question 1 - Three differences between web1, web2, web3

As we saw in class, each one of web1, web2, web3 are different generations of the world wide web, each one have name that summarized their goal:
web1 "The read-only web",
web2 "The participative social web",
web3 "The read, write, execute web"

The first difference between the three is the user participations.
when in web1 he can read,
in web2 he can read and write - information is exchanged among users on social media platforms,
and in web3 the use of the user in the web is more portable and personal - adapted to the individual needs.

The second difference is the focus of each web.
In web1 the focus is on the company publishes the information,
in web2 the focus is on the community, thanks to the to user's ability to exchange content,
in web3 the focus in on the individual since the use of the user in the web is more portable and personal - adapted to the individual needs.

The third difference is the goal of the web.
In web1 information sharing is the main goal, therefore the inforamtion is mostly static.
In web2 interaction is the main goal, it aims to connect people and introduction of web applications.
In web3 the goal is to create "smart web" based of the needs of the individual, where the same web/data can be shown differently to different users.

Question 2 - use case example to demonstrate the differences mentioned above

for the query - "Hotels in Amsterdam"
We expect that in all networks the result will be a list of hotels in Amsterdam, but the content itself will be completely different.
In web1, the outcome will be static list of hotels in Amsterdam, when the list will be built mainly based on publications of the hotels themselves.
In web2, the list will be built mainly based on different users of the web, thing that allow users to share experiences and reviews about hotels.
For example, we can see rates of hotels, photos, expereiences of other users, hence we get more reliable information about hotels in Amsterdam.
In web3, the list will be mainly adapted to the user who performed the search. For example it could be ranked by users reviews, adapted to hotels I visited in and enjoyed the stay or hotels I recently viewd and show interest,
Intrest can be evident in several aspects based on different algorithms, such as time I spent in the web page,
"LIKE" buttons I clicked, pages I returned to several times and more.
In conclusion, In web1 the result will be static and factual information,
in web2 the information will be based in sharing informations between different users,
and in web 3 we will recieve results that are adapted to the need and nature of the searcher's search.
